<font:Arial Bold:32>Notation - Rests<font:Arial:16>
<lmargin%:8>
If you want to add pauses between notes, you can use rests.

  <font:Arial Bold:18>$<font:Arial:16> adds a pause equal to a single note
  <font:Arial Bold:18>-<font:Arial:16> adds a pause equal to <font:Arial Bold:16>2 <font:Arial:16>notes
  <font:Arial Bold:18>_<font:Arial:16> adds a pause equal to <font:Arial Bold:16>4 <font:Arial:16>notes
  <font:Arial Bold:18>/<font:Arial:16> adds a pause equal to <font:Arial Bold:16>1/2 <font:Arial:16>of a note
  <font:Arial Bold:18>;<font:Arial:16> adds a pause equal to <font:Arial Bold:16>1/4 <font:Arial:16>of a note

Example:
<lmargin%:10>
<font:Arial Bold:20>C4<color:FF0000>$<color:000000> , D4<color:FF0000>-<color:000000> , E4<color:FF0000>_<color:000000> , F4<color:FF0000>/<color:000000> , G5<color:FF0000>;<color:000000> , A4
<font:Arial:16><lmargin%:8>

You can also combine them to create oddly-timed rests.

Example:
<lmargin%:10>
<font:Arial Bold:20>C4 <color:FF0000>$/<color:000000>, D4 <color:FF0000>;-_$<color:000000>, E4 <color:FF0000>/_<color:000000>, F4 <color:FF0000>;/<color:000000>, G5 <color:FF0000>;$<color:000000>, A4
